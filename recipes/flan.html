Spanish Flan

Description

The Spanish Flan has been said to trace back all the way to the Roman Empire

and has always been a favorite Latin desert. It was eventually populated by

European culture, due to the Spanish, but now people from all over the world enjoy

an amazing dish of flan!

Ingredients

  • 3 Eggs
  • 1 cup white sugar
  • 1 (14 ounce) can sweetened condensed milk
  • 1 (12 fluid ounce) can evaporated milk
  • 1 tablespoon vanilla extract

Directions

    Step 1

  1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C)

    2. Step 2

  2. In a medium saucepan over medium-low heat, melt sugar until liquefied

    and golden in color. Carefully pour hot syrup into a 9 inch round glass baking dish,

    turning the dish to evenly coat the bottom and sides. Set aside

    3. Step 3

  3. In a large bowl, beat eggs. Beat in condensed milk, evaporated milk and vanilla until smooth

    4. Step 4

  4. Bake in preheated oven 60 minutes. Let cool completeley

    5. Step 5

  5. To serve, carefully invert on serving plate with edges when completely cool.
